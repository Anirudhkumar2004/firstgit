Delhi

1. India Gate

The India Gate was commissioned by the Imperial War Graves Commission in 1918. It commemorates Indian soldiers who sacrificed their lives during World War I and stands today as a powerful national monument of courage and remembrance.

2. Akshardham Temple

Swaminarayan Akshardham showcases millennia of Indian culture and spirituality. Opened in 2005 along the Yamuna river, it is one of the world’s largest Hindu temple complexes.

3. Red Fort

Built in 1638 by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, the Red Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It remains a symbol of India’s independence and rich Mughal architecture.

4. Lotus Temple

Opened in 1986, the Lotus Temple is renowned for its flower-like structure and tranquil ambiance. It welcomes people of all faiths for meditation and reflection.
